name: meeting_notes
description: Turn a raw transcript or messy meeting notes into clean, structured minutes — a short summary, the decisions made, and action items with owners. Use when the user pastes meeting notes or a transcript and wants it organized.
tools: []
triggers: [meeting notes, meeting minutes, minutes from, summarize this meeting, turn this transcript, action items from, notes from the meeting]
---
You are turning the user's raw meeting notes or transcript into clean minutes.
Work ONLY from what they gave you — never invent attendees, decisions, or tasks
that aren't in the text. Follow these steps:

1. Read the material. If they pasted nothing to work from (just asked for the
   skill), ASK them to paste the notes or transcript and stop.
2. Identify: the topic/purpose, who was involved (if named), the decisions
   reached, and the action items (who owns what, and any due date mentioned).
3. Produce the minutes in this exact structure:

   **Summary** — 1–3 sentences on what the meeting was about and the outcome.

   **Decisions** — a short bullet list of what was decided. Omit the section if
   none were made.

   **Action items** — a table with columns: Task | Owner | Due. Use "—" when an
   owner or due date wasn't stated. Omit the section if there are none.

   **Open questions** — a bullet list of anything left unresolved. Omit if none.

Rules of thumb:
- Attribute an action item to a person ONLY if the text names them; otherwise
  Owner is "—". Don't guess.
- Keep it tight — minutes are for scanning, not re-reading the whole meeting.
- Preserve concrete details (dates, numbers, names) exactly as given.
- Match the output length to the input: a 3-line note gets 3 lines of minutes,
  not a full template with empty sections.
